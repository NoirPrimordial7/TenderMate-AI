# Reference direction

The references are pattern sources only. TenderMate will not copy their layouts, assets, typefaces, source code, or branded interactions.

## Iventions — 55% / adopt and remix

Sources: [Iventions](https://iventions.com/), [Iventions on its Website of the Month](https://iventions.com/insight/iventions-awarded-with-website-of-the-month), and [CSS Design Awards listing](https://www.cssdesignawards.com/woty2025/sites/iventions/).

Observed: the site leads with “Step into the Spotlight,” pairs a direct headline with a visual stage, and describes its own system as clean lines, confident/cinematic motion, crisp typography, and “precision with a pulse.” CSS Design Awards categorises it as animated, colourful, and typographic.

Take:

- One unmistakable first-viewport scene rather than a stack of content sections.
- Large typography as an active compositional surface.
- Spotlight contrast and foreground/background scene changes.
- Staged entrance: navigation, label, headline masks, engine object, then control dock.
- Strong transitions built around a clear narrative, not independent decorative animation.
- Square/industrial control geometry and confident short copy.

Translate for TenderMate:

- The “spotlight” becomes a lime intelligence beam crossing a carbon document engine.
- Event spectacle becomes document certainty: “KNOW BEFORE YOU BID.”
- Cinematic movement is confined to the initial reveal, auth/upload activation, and low-frequency engine loop.
- The real form and upload workflow remains immediately usable and never waits for an animation.

Avoid:

- Showreel/video dependence, scroll-led narrative, oversized cursor effects, and event-brand imagery.

## Krea — 20% / adopt

Sources: [Krea](https://www.krea.ai/) and [Krea redesign notes](https://www.krea.ai/blog/redesign).

Observed: Krea describes a work-first redesign with one navigational system, live indicators, scannable model metadata, hidden secondary controls, removed animation delays, persistent sessions, and a mobile experience rebuilt for mobile rather than compressed from desktop.

Take:

- A compact powered control dock with clear modes and high information density.
- Technical labels that explain state, cost/usage, and capability without visual clutter.
- Progressive disclosure and strong defaults.
- Fast UI timing and a dedicated mobile composition.

Translate for TenderMate:

- Authentication and upload occupy the same dock shell.
- Plan and credit values appear as compact real-data rows.
- File selection and upload state replace the form without spawning extra cards.
- Secondary privacy/daily-limit information stays subordinate but readable.

Avoid:

- Copying Krea’s application navigation, model selector, canvas layout, or product language.

## Spline — 15% / remix

Sources: [Spline](https://spline.design/) and [Spline documentation](https://docs.spline.design/basics/what-is-spline).

Observed: Spline emphasises interactive spatial experiences, state/event-driven motion, and direct manipulation. Its website invites pointer interaction with “Press and drag to interact.”

Take:

- A central object with layered depth and clear foreground/midground/background planes.
- Small pointer-responsive depth on capable desktop devices.
- State-aware visual reactions when auth activates or a file enters the dropzone.

Translate for TenderMate:

- Use CSS 3D transforms, SVG/grid layers, and DOM papers instead of adding WebGL.
- Parallax is capped to a few pixels, never required for comprehension, and disabled for touch/reduced motion.
- The document engine is semantically decorative; real status is communicated in the control dock.

Avoid:

- Heavy 3D runtime, drag-to-understand interactions, physics, and GPU-intensive effects.

## Nothing — 10% / remix

Source: [Nothing About](https://us.nothing.tech/about).

Observed: Nothing describes a transparent design language that reveals decisions beneath the surface and draws from 90s rave culture and aerospace materials while keeping interactions intentionally quiet.

Take:

- Exposed grid, registration marks, identifiers, and component seams.
- Monospace microcopy, uppercase system labels, sparse signal red, and black/white discipline.
- Mechanical clarity: the document engine should show how it reads clauses rather than hiding behind a generic AI symbol.

Translate for TenderMate:

- OCR frames, clause coordinates, source-verified stamps, scan indices, and paper-layer construction.
- Carbon and paper-white are structural; electric lime is the primary active signal, ultraviolet adds depth, cyan is reserved for secondary data, and red is error-only.

Avoid:

- Copying Nothing’s dot-matrix branding, glyphs, transparent hardware motifs, or phone UI.

## Synthesised direction

- Adopt: Iventions scene choreography; Krea control density; Nothing technical legibility.
- Remix: Spline spatial depth into a lightweight DOM document object.
- Avoid: reference-specific assets, scroll theatre, branded type treatments, and any motion that delays sign-in or upload.
